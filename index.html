About Me

My Journey

My journey into technology began with a simple curiosity about how computers work. I didn't grow up with access to high-end gear, but I always loved learning about how things functioned behind the screen. That quiet interest stayed with me — even as life took me in other directions.

After spending time in jobs that didn't challenge or inspire me, I realized it was time to pursue something I truly cared about. That spark led me to Lethbridge Polytechnic, where I fully committed to learning the hands-on skills I'd always been drawn to — networking, cybersecurity, server setups, and hardware.

Working at Shaw and Fido gave me a chance to apply what I was learning and connect it to real people. I learned that tech isn't just about fixing systems — it's about helping people feel understood, supported, and empowered through technology.

Today, I bring that mindset to everything I build. Whether I'm setting up secure systems, solving hardware challenges, or refining user-facing tools, I aim to make tech that's reliable, practical, and built with people in mind.
